Social Impact

We advance sustainability, cultivate inclusion and transform lives with technology.

At KUMO Tech, we are committed to driving human progress.

Progress Made Real shows how we will create a positive and lasting social impact on humankind and the planet through 2030 — using our reach, technology and people.

Through our annual reporting, we demonstrate how we are making this progress real.

Advancing Sustainability

We have a responsibility to protect and enrich our planet together with our customers, suppliers and communities. It is a core part of our business and we embed sustainability and ethical practices into all that we do, being accountable for our actions while driving improvements wherever and whenever possible.

01 / 03

KUMO TECH works with employers to help millions of people live more securely and with dignity.

02 / 03

We help finance breakthroughs in education, science and the environment by offering a wide range of solutions and products to help them achieve their goals regardless of time horizon.

03 / 03

We help countries build their infrastructure, implement monetary policies and make savings for future generations by working with central banks, governments and sovereign wealth funds.

Right now our main focus is Asian market, and the first point of our technological adventure is Malaysia - country of numerous opportunities and serious challenges that we are eager to overcome, helping the local population, business and government thrive and utilize modern technologies at best.

01 / 03

For instance, 5G is not just about enhanced speeds and connectivity, but it’s also an economic driver that can improve the quality of life, businesses including SMEs, and the government.

02 / 03

According to the Malaysian Institute of Economic Research (MIER), 5G implementation will a significant impact on the nation’s economy. It is estimated that it would contribute up to RM12.7 billion to the GDP between 2021 and 2025. On top of that, it has the potential to create more than 39,000 new jobs higher income, and almost 40 per cent of these jobs are expected to be available in 2025.
